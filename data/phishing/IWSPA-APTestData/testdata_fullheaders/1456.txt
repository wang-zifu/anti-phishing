‘Eleanor Rigby’s Ned Benson Q&A, TIFF Spotlight: ‘The Drop’s Michael R. Roskam, Top 10 Screenwriting Blogs, Top 10 Screenwriting Books



SSN ORIGINALS



Filmmaker Ned Benson Takes SSN Behind the Visual Style of 'The Disappearance of Eleanor Rigby'
Benson’s work here is complex, rich, and visually striking—not in over-the-top ways, but in subtle, deeply affecting ways. SSN Insider talked to Benson about creating the look of the films, shooting in New York, and the scene he’s most proud of. (SSN) 



TIFF Spotlight: The Drop's Michael R. Roskam Reveals the Particular Way He Directed Gandolfini & Hardy "I Don’t do Rehearsals" 
Belgian director Michael R. Roskam’s latest is based on the short story Animal Rescue, by Dennis Lehane (Mystic River, Gone Baby Gone, Shutter Island), who also penned the script. SSN Insider spoke to Roskam about his Toronto experience, how he works with actors, and why no scene is ever easy to shoot. (SSN) 



The Top 10 Most Influential Screenwriting Blogs
Stephanie Palmer presents, in no particular order, the screenwriting blogs screenwriters should be following, along with excerpts from each, including Scott Myers, Jeanne Veillette Bowerman, Erik Bork and Jen Grisanti. (SSN) 



Top 10 Screenwriting Books Screenwriters Should Read
These ten books for screenwriters are the ones that agents, managers, executives, stars, and directors reference the most.  From Syd Field to David Mamet and William Goldman, the list starts here (SSN) 



TODAY’S HEADLINES



‘TIFF has become an ongoing reminder of the talent Hollywood is failing to capture — "Why aren't you hiring any of these women?"
The Week writes that Toronto is the best platform for female directors, as evidenced by the 58 who represented their work at this year’s fest. Here’s a look at some of the stand-outs to remember. (TW) 



Louis C.K.’s Tao of Getting Older
Uproxx’s homage to the comedian as he turns 47 today, with a guide to aging from 15 to 60, in 5-year intervals. (UP) 



Trailer Buzz: ‘Serena’
The 2:03 trailer for the Jennifer Lawrence and Bradley Cooper starrer about newlyweds who attempt to start a lumber empire in Depression-era North Carolina -  but when she finds out her husband has had a child outside of their marriage, their intense, passionate bond spins out of control. Premieres at the London Film Fest next month. Susanne Bier directed. (DH) 



Real-Life Pitch Tales: “Don’t blow off meetings. You never know when you might meet the right person at the right time.”
The Hollywood Pitching Bible authors present a pitching story from the book’s second edition, centered on how Paul Guay successfully pitched Liar, Liar to Brian Grazer. (HJ) 



Trailer Buzz: ‘John Wick’
Keanu Reeves stars in this 2:32 trailer as an ex-hitman who comes out of retirement to track down the gangsters that took everything from him. The film opens October 24 and co-stars Adrianne Palicki, Michael Nyqvist, Willem Dafoe, Ian McShane and Dean Winters. (COLL)
