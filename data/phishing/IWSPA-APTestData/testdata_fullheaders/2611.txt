Top Stories

1. EU fines Citigroup, JPMorgan, RBS and three other banks a record $2.34B for colluding to fix key interest rate benchmarks, placing largest fine (over $980M) on Deutsche Bank.       

2. Not a great week for Deutsche Bank: in Hong Kong, a former managing director was sentenced to seven years in prison for accepting $3.2M in bribes. Meanwhile, an employee of the bank's Japanese domain.com has been arrested on suspicion of bribery in the form of excessive entertainment.      

3. German engineering company Bilfinger to pay $32M in FCPA deferred prosecution agreement over charges of bribing Nigerian officials to win gas contracts. Bilfinger has three years to improve its controls; CEO notes that the company is already implementing "a modern and efficient system."
