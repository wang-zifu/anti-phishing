SSN Emmy Originals: Writing & Directing - Drama & Comedy Series Analyis, Spotlight Q&A’s: Moira Walley-Beckett, Mike Judge, Carl Franklin

Outstanding Writing & Directing – Drama, Comedy Series



The Emmy Handicap: Female Favorites May Make History in Writing & Directing Categories
SSN’s quick handicap of this year’s Emmy writing and directing nominees, in both drama and comedy series, starts here. (SSN) 

Emmy Spotlight: The Single Greatest Hour of TV? Moira Walley-Beckett on Writing the Breaking Bad Episode "Ozymandias"
Breaking Bad’s third-to-last episode, Ozymandias, is widely hailed as the best the show has to offer. Written by Moira Walley-Beckett, she is now in production with the new Starz drama Flesh and Bone - her first time as creator and show runner. SSN’s interview with the Emmy nominee is here (SSN) 

Emmy Spotlight: How an Engineering Background Helped Mike Judge Craft Silicon Valley
Judge studied engineering and worked in Silicon Valley for a brief time before Beavis and Butt-Head sprung from his imagination, so tech is a world he knows a bit about. SSN spoke with Judge about casting the show, how his engineering background has helped him in his career, and the toughest casting battle he’s had to fight. (SSN) 

Emmy Spotlight: House of Cards' Carl Franklin on Directing Kevin Spacey & the Best Advice He's Ever Received
Franklin has directed several episodes of the political drama, but started off in front of the camera before stepping behind the lens on features like One False Move and Devil in a Blue Dress. SSN spoke to Franklin about his most memorable moments on Cards, working with Kevin Spacey and Robin Wright, and how he found out about his nomination. (SSN)

TV Development For 8/5/14: Wolfgang Petersen & Scott Stuber To Exec Produce 'Ghost Brigades' For Syfy
SSN’s weekly TV production charts with the latest projects in television development. This week’s titles includes: Big Kids, Debt and the Untitled Richard Price Crime Drama. (SSN) 

Kurt Sutter’s Open Letter to the Creative Community — Dear Family, Friends and Enemies:
Variety’s guest columnist post features the letter from Sons of Anarchy’s Kurt Sutter on his fight with Google: “Don’t worry, I’m not asking you to donate to a fucking Kickstarter campaign. All I want is to make you aware of what’s happening in the streaming backrooms of the virtual boys club.” (VAR) 

21st Century Fox Withdraws Time Warner Bid
Nikki Finke writes of the withdrawn offer along with the probable reasoning behind it: “The score is Jeff Bewkes 1, Rupert Murdoch 0.” (NF) 

What’s In A Title? Producers: The Hierarchy Explained
Script’s piece on what exactly the myriad producer titles entail, from Associate Producer to Assistant Producer, as well as Just Producer, Bi-Polar Executive Producer and more. (SCRIPT) 

HarperCollins’ Digital Crime Fiction Imprint, Killer Reads, Accepting Unagented Manuscripts
Galleycat on the news of the submission window for unagented manuscripts, in English and a minimum of 70,000 words. HarperCollins UK will be accepting submissions for its planned release of two titles per month from August 29 through September 14. The link to send your manuscript is included in the article here (GC) 

TV Buzz:  ‘Drunk History’
The 2:57 preview clip from tonight’s episode of the Comedy Central series tackles the making of Citizen Kane, starring Jack Black and John Lithgow in the re-enactment. (GAW)
