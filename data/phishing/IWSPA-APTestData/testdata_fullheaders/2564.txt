STEM's Impact on U.S. Global Competitiveness 

The Congressional Hispanic Leadership Institute (CHLI), will host a panel to discuss Science, Technology, Engineering and Math (STEM) education and its impact on U.S. Global Competitiveness. CHLI is honored to welcome these experts:

Joedis Avila 
Manager of Community Outreach 
Ford Motor Company

Joe has made a career in corporate social responsibility, international business and multicultural marketing. In his position, he manages Ford's strategic partnerships with key organization and external corporate initiatives on a national and local level. He also manages strategic investments in Hispanic markets.

Mary Ann GomezExecutive Director CHLI (Moderator)Mary Ann brings to CHLI years of experience in marketing, public affairs, and community service.An educate advocate, she is also an adjunct professor at American University. The CHLI Trade & International Affairs Symposium was created under her leadership. 



Hillary MaxwellDirector of Communications Dell, Inc.Hillary manages global public affairs efforts for Dell. With over a decade of experience in strategic communications and public affairs. Prior to joining Dell, Hillary was a political appointee at the U.S. Department of Education where she served as a Regional Director in the Office of Strategic Communications. 

Dr. Mark RosenbergPresident Florida International University (FIU)The first FIU faculty member to ascend to the University's presidency, Dr. Rosenberg has over 35 years of experience in higher education leadership. He was one of the principal architects of FIU's growth during the past decade, and is the author of several scholarly articles on Latin America.
